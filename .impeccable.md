## Design Context

### Users
Two overlapping audiences: casual hobbyists who knit/crochet for relaxation (beginners to intermediate, value simplicity and inspiration) and serious crafters with large stashes and complex multi-project needs (value power, organization, and depth). The interface must feel welcoming to beginners without being dumbed down for experts. Use progressive disclosure — keep the surface simple, let power users dig deeper.

Core jobs to be done:
- Discover and save knitting/crochet patterns
- Log and track works-in-progress (WIPs)
- Manage yarn stash (inventory, colorways, yardage)
- Record finished objects with photos and notes
- Find community and inspiration from other crafters

### Brand Personality
Warm, creative, tactile. Like a beautifully curated yarn shop — welcoming, inspiring, and deeply knowledgeable without being intimidating.

Three-word personality: **Warm. Tactile. Inviting.**

Emotional goals: users should feel calm, inspired, and at home. The app is a creative sanctuary — a place to escape into a beloved craft. It should feel like flipping through a beautiful craft magazine while wrapped in a cozy blanket.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Warm & cozy** — earthy, organic, and handmade-feeling without being rustic or dated.

Visual tone:
- Earthy palette: warm creams, terracottas, dusty roses, sage greens, deep walnuts — the colors of natural yarn
- Typography: editorial display fonts paired with readable body text; hints of craft/editorial publishing
- Textures: subtle paper or linen-like backgrounds; avoid cold, flat digital surfaces
- Photography-forward: yarn, needles, hands knitting, finished garments — imagery is central
- Light mode primary — the warmth doesn't work in dark mode

Anti-references:
- **Not Ravelry**: no cluttered tables, dense navigation, or 2008-era information architecture
- **Not generic SaaS**: no blue-gradient dashboards, data cards with rounded corners, or productivity-app chrome
- **Not craft-kitsch**: avoid "grandma's craft room" clichés — no yarn ball clip art, no Comic Sans energy, no pastel overload

Inspiration touchpoints: Kinfolk magazine (warm editorial photography), Etsy's product pages (tactile warmth), a well-curated independent yarn shop's website.

### Design Principles

1. **Craft is the hero** — yarn, texture, and finished objects should always be the visual focal point. The UI serves the content, never competes with it.

2. **Warm but refined** — cozy doesn't mean chaotic. Clean layouts, generous whitespace, and precise typography give warmth a sophisticated container.

3. **Progressive complexity** — the surface is simple and inviting for beginners; depth (advanced stash management, pattern notation, row counters) reveals through interaction for power users.

4. **Handmade soul in a digital medium** — typography, texture, and color choices should evoke the physicality of yarn and fiber. This app should feel materially different from software.

5. **No SaaS DNA** — avoid every visual cue that signals "productivity tool": no metric cards, no status badges, no stepper-form chrome, no dashboard layouts. This is a creative companion, not a project management app.
